# Intervention library

Use this library to pick one intervention and deliver it concretely. Do not dump the whole library into a user response.

## 1. Soft start-up builder

Use for raising a concern without blame.

Steps:

1. Convert global complaint into observable event.
2. Name one feeling.
3. State a positive need.
4. Make a small request.
5. Stop after one issue.

Template:

> “When [specific event] happened, I felt [emotion]. I need/would appreciate [positive need]. Could we [small request]?”

Example:

> “When the plans changed and I found out last minute, I felt unimportant. I need more notice when possible. Could you text me as soon as you know plans are shifting?”

## 2. Listener-summary drill

Use when partners interrupt, rebut, or feel unheard.

Rules:

- Speaker gets two minutes.
- Listener may only summarise and validate.
- Speaker corrects the summary.
- Switch roles.
- Problem-solving starts only after both feel understood.

Listener phrases:

- “What I hear is...”
- “That makes sense because...”
- “The part I didn’t realise was...”
- “Did I get it?”

## 3. Time-out and return agreement

Use for flooding, shutdown, or escalation.

Script:

> “I’m flooded. I’m taking 30 minutes so I don’t make this worse. I will come back at [time]. I’m not abandoning the conversation.”

Return script:

> “I’m back. The part I understand is [summary]. The part I still need to explain is [one sentence].”

## 4. Regrettable incident repair

Use after yelling, contempt, defensiveness, withdrawal, or a bad fight.

Prompts:

- What did I do that I regret?
- What impact might it have had?
- What was I feeling underneath?
- What can I own without excuses?
- What will I do differently next time?
- What does my partner need before they can reconnect?

Apology template:

> “I’m sorry for [specific behaviour]. I can see it affected you by [impact]. I was feeling [context], but that doesn’t excuse it. Next time I will [specific behaviour]. You don’t have to make me feel better about it right now.”

## 5. Four Horsemen conversion

Use when the user gives a harsh draft.

- Criticism to complaint: “You’re lazy” becomes “I feel overloaded when the laundry stays in the basket.”
- Contempt to respect: “You’re pathetic” becomes “I’m too angry to speak respectfully; I need a break.”
- Defensiveness to ownership: “It’s not my fault” becomes “I can own that I didn’t tell you sooner.”
- Stonewalling to self-soothing: silence becomes “I need 30 minutes and I will return.”

## 7. Connection-before-analysis reset

Use when talking about the relationship repeatedly escalates.

Plan:

- 20 minutes of connection with no analysis.
- One appreciation each.
- Shared easy activity.
- No complaint during reset.
- Later, ten minutes on one issue.

Script:

> “I think we’ve made talking itself feel dangerous. Tonight I don’t want to analyse us. I want us to have tea, be kind for 20 minutes, and then stop.”

## 8. Dreams-under-position interview

Use for recurring unsolved issues.

Questions:

- What does your position mean to you?
- What value or fear is underneath it?
- What did your family teach you about this?
- What would feel disrespectful or scary about giving in?
- What part could be flexible?
- What would make you feel understood even if we disagree?

End with:

> “One part of your dream I respect is...”

## 9. Compromise circles

Use after dream-under-position work.

Inner circle:

- Core needs.
- Values.
- Non-negotiables.

Outer circle:

- Preferences.
- Timing.
- Method.
- Degree.
- Trial period.

Agreement:

> “For two weeks we will try [specific overlap]. We review on [date].”

## 10. Love Maps mini-date

Use for distance, roommate feeling, or low curiosity.

Questions:

- What has been taking up the most space in your mind?
- What are you looking forward to?
- What are you worried about?
- What do you wish I understood about this season of your life?
- What is one small way I could make this week easier?

Rule: no fixing unless requested.

## 11. Bids audit

Use when one partner feels ignored.

Track for two days:

- Bid made.
- Response: toward, away, against.
- What got in the way.
- Better micro-response.

Micro-responses:

- Look up.
- Smile.
- Ask one question.
- Touch shoulder if welcome.
- Say “I’m busy, but I want to hear this in ten minutes.”

## 12. Appreciation ratio reset

Use when negativity dominates.

Each day for one week, name one specific behaviour you appreciated.

Bad appreciation:

- “You’re great.”

Better:

- “I noticed you made the appointment without me asking. That made me feel less alone in the admin.”

## 13. State of the relationship meeting

Use for weekly maintenance, not active escalation.

Agenda:

1. Appreciations.
2. What went well.
3. One issue only.
4. Partner A speaks, B summarises.
5. Partner B speaks, A summarises.
6. Each owns one small part.
7. One experiment.
8. Connection close.

Rules:

- No surprise ambush.
- No more than 30 minutes at first.
- If flooded, pause and return.
- No contempt.

## 14. Trust-repair observables

Use for betrayal or broken agreements.

Ask:

- What agreement was broken?
- What information is needed to restore reality?
- What behaviour will stop?
- What behaviour will start?
- How long is the review period?
- What is transparent without becoming surveillance?

Examples:

- “I will tell you before contact with X, not after.”
- “I will not delete messages related to this issue for the next month.”
- “We will discuss triggers Sunday for 20 minutes.”

## 15. Boundary script

Use when the user needs a clear limit.

Template:

> “I’m willing to [constructive behaviour]. I’m not willing to [harmful behaviour]. If [boundary violation] happens, I will [self-protective action].”

Example:

> “I’m willing to talk about chores tonight. I’m not willing to be called names. If name-calling starts, I’ll pause the conversation and return tomorrow.”

## 20. Pleasure equity reset

Use for orgasm gap, penetration-centred sex, or one partner feeling like sex is not for them.

Principles:

- The receiver’s feedback matters more than generic tips.
- Sex does not need to centre penetration.
- Clitoral stimulation, pacing, comfort, and context often matter.
- Pleasure is not a performance test.

Script:

> “I want our sex to feel good for both of us. Can we spend a few times exploring what you enjoy without making orgasm the goal?”

## 23. CNC discussion map

Use for consensual non-consent fantasy or roleplay discussion.

Questions:

- Is this fantasy-only or something you want to discuss trying?
- What emotional tone appeals: surrender, pursuit, taboo, being desired, loss of control, power, danger-feeling without danger?
- What words/actions are off-limits?
- What stops the scene immediately?
- What aftercare is required?
- What would make this feel unsafe?
- How will you distinguish roleplay from real no?

Do not write erotic assault scripts. Keep it negotiation-focused.

## 24. Flirting message builder

Use for dating or rekindling flirtation.

Formula:

- Specific observation.
- Warm tone.
- Light play or sincere appreciation.
- Clear invitation.

Examples:

- “Your voice note made my morning. Want to continue this over coffee Thursday?”
- “I like how easy this feels with you. Are you free for a drink this weekend?”
- “I’m attracted to you, and I don’t want to be vague about it. Want to go on a date?”

## 25. Rejection-respect script

Use after no or ambiguity.

> “Thanks for being clear. I’m disappointed, but I respect it. No hard feelings.”

Then stop pursuing unless they re-initiate clearly.

## 26. Love languages care menu

Use as vocabulary, not rigid theory.

Have each partner choose current top two from:

- Words of affirmation.
- Quality time.
- Acts of service.
- Gifts or tokens.
- Physical touch.

Then make each concrete:

- “Words” becomes “Tell me one thing you appreciated about how I handled today.”
- “Quality time” becomes “Twenty minutes with phones away.”
- “Acts” becomes “Take the bins out without me tracking it.”
- “Gifts” becomes “Bring me a coffee when you get one.”
- “Touch” becomes “Hug me for 20 seconds when we get home.”

## 27. Stay-or-leave clarity matrix

Use when no immediate safety red flags are present.

Columns:

- What is painful now?
- What have I already tried?
- What has changed in observable behaviour?
- What has only been promised?
- What would need to change by a specific date?
- What am I responsible for?
- What is my partner responsible for?
- What support do I need?

If safety red flags exist, use safety planning instead.

## 28. Post-conflict debrief

Use after a fight when both are calm.

Questions:

- What triggered me?
- What did I feel underneath anger or shutdown?
- What did I do that made it worse?
- What did you hear me trying to say?
- What do I understand about your side?
- What repair do I owe?
- What is one change for next time?

## 29. Repair text message

Use for immediate repair by text.

> “I don’t like how I handled that. I got defensive and then blamed you. I’m going to take a bit to calm down, but I care about this and want to restart more respectfully later.”

## 30. One-partner-only coaching

Use when the partner is absent.

Focus on:

- User’s words.
- User’s regulation.
- User’s boundaries.
- User’s repair.
- User’s next decision.

Avoid:

- Diagnosing partner.
- Assuming partner’s motives.
- Creating a script that corners the partner.

Final line:

> “You can control the clarity and respect of your ask; you can’t control whether they respond maturely.”
